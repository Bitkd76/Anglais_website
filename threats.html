The stakes

Threats facing
our living world

Understanding the forces driving biodiversity loss is the first step toward reversing them.

Three major crises

Why species are disappearing

🔥

Habitat Destruction

Deforestation, urbanisation and intensive agriculture are dismantling natural environments faster than at any point in human history. Every hour, thousands of hectares of irreplaceable habitat vanish. Species lose their homes — and with them, their future.

🌡️

Climate Change

Rising temperatures shift seasonal rhythms, push species beyond their viable ranges and bleach coral reefs — ecosystems that support 25% of all marine life. Climate change amplifies every other threat, creating cascading collapses.

☠️

Pollution & Overexploitation

Pesticides, microplastics, industrial runoff and poaching push thousands of species toward extinction each year — often before they have even been scientifically described. We are erasing life faster than we can document it.

By the numbers

A crisis in figures

📉

1,000,000+

Species currently threatened with extinction, according to the IPBES Global Assessment — the most comprehensive review of biodiversity loss ever conducted.

📊

69%

Decline in global wildlife populations since 1970, as measured by the WWF Living Planet Index. Two thirds of vertebrate life, gone in half a century.

🌲

1,600 ha / hour

Rate of forest destruction worldwide. That is 15 million hectares of forest lost every single year — an area larger than England.

🐝

40%

Of insect species are in decline. Insects pollinate 75% of our food crops — their loss would trigger a food security crisis of unprecedented scale.

🌊

50%

Of coral reefs have been lost since 1950. At current rates, 90% will be gone by 2050 — taking with them a quarter of all marine biodiversity.

⏱️

1,000× faster

The current extinction rate compared to the natural background rate. Scientists call this the Sixth Mass Extinction — and unlike the previous five, this one is caused by us.
