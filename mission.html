Our purpose

Protecting what
keeps us alive

Biodiversity is not merely an aesthetic concern — it is the very fabric of our survival. BioFriendly exists to defend it.

🌍

What we believe

Nature sustains
everything we are

Forests purify our air. Oceans regulate our climate. Insects pollinate our crops. Wetlands filter our water. Each ecosystem is a life-support system — and right now, they are failing.

As university students, we are part of the generation that will bear the consequences of today's choices. That is why we created BioFriendly — to act where we can, educate where we must, and fund the science that proves what is at stake.

Our actions

What we actually do

🌱

Reforestation

We partner with local NGOs to plant native trees in critical deforestation zones, prioritising biodiversity over monoculture.

🐠

Marine Protection

Funding reef monitoring and restoration programs — because coral reefs shelter 25% of all marine species despite covering less than 1% of the ocean floor.

🔬

Scientific Support

Backing field researchers who document species before they disappear — because you cannot protect what you have not yet named.

📚

Education

Running awareness workshops on campus and in local communities, making biodiversity science accessible to everyone.

🤝

Community Engagement

Working with local populations whose livelihoods depend on healthy ecosystems — conservation that includes people, not just nature.

📢

Advocacy

Amplifying the voices of scientists and indigenous communities in policy discussions that shape the future of our natural world.

Our values

Built on three pillars

🧬

Science-first

Every decision we make is grounded in peer-reviewed research and expert consensus. No greenwashing, no shortcuts.

🌐

Transparency

We publish annual reports detailing exactly where every euro goes. Your trust is our most valuable resource.

🔋

Long-term thinking

Biodiversity loss is a slow emergency. We are in this for the decades ahead — not just the next news cycle.
